# Steven Christopher — Brand Skill

This file is the canonical brand brief for the Steven Christopher artist
project. Concrete UI constraints (palette, type, pacing, anti-patterns) are
mirrored in `.cursor/rules/brand.mdc` and load automatically when editing
files under `src/`. This skill loads the deeper narrative on demand.

## How to use this skill

1. **Identify which sections apply** to the current task:
   - Copy / bio / press → *Brand Personality*, *Core Brand Identity*, *Strategic Brand Principle*
   - Image selection or generation → *Visual Identity*, *Photography Direction*, *Color Direction*
   - Hero / video / motion → *Website Direction*, *Visual Identity*, *Overall Creative Directive*
   - Campaign or concept work → *Artistic Positioning*, *Strategic Brand Principle*
2. **Pressure-test the output** against the central tension: *reckless intimacy
   vs emotional distance*. Visuals carry vulnerability and atmosphere; music
   and lyrics carry the recklessness. If the work collapses one side into the
   other, it is off-brand.
3. **Vibe-check before shipping.** The result should feel like *a quiet scene
   from an indie film at 2am rather than a polished mainstream pop campaign.*
   If it feels manufactured, performative, or sentimental, revise.

## Output posture

Short, declarative, slightly withholding. Confident without overstatement.
Leave space for the audience to lean in.

# Brand Brief

## Core Brand Identity

Steven Christopher is a cinematic R&B artist whose identity lives in the
tension between recklessness and vulnerability. The project explores intimacy,
desire, emotional ambiguity, and solitary late-night experiences through a
grounded, minimal, and emotionally charged lens.

The music exists in a world where lust, detachment, and unexpected emotional
connection collide. Themes often center around modern relationships, casual
sex, emotional imbalance, catching feelings unexpectedly, and navigating
emotional consequences without sentimentality.

The tone is intimate but emotionally restrained — confident without arrogance,
vulnerable without melodrama.

The overall feeling should be:

- cinematic
- nocturnal
- nostalgic
- sensual
- detached
- emotionally unresolved
- minimal
- editorial
- grounded in reality

The project should feel like:

> a quiet scene from an indie film at 2am rather than a polished mainstream pop campaign.

---

## Artistic Positioning

### Adjacent Artists

Use these as directional references, not imitation:

- Brent Faiyaz — emotional detachment, intimacy, modern toxicity
- Frank Ocean — introspection, cinematic vulnerability
- Prince — sensuality, confidence, artistry
- Pharrell Williams — taste, creative direction, multi-disciplinary artistry

### Explicit Differentiation

The brand should intentionally avoid:

- glossy pop aesthetics
- overproduction
- hyper-commercial energy
- overly polished "industry" visuals
- obvious influencer aesthetics

The project should feel:

- more raw
- more personal
- more minimal
- more emotionally believable

Compared to artists like Justin Bieber, Steven Christopher should feel less
manufactured and more organically cinematic.

---

## Visual Identity

### Visual World

The visual environment is:

- evening/nighttime
- solitary
- urban or emotionally isolated
- grainy
- monochromatic or heavily desaturated
- realistic rather than fantastical

Primary inspiration references:

- A24 film aesthetics
- Euphoria cinematography and mood, but stripped down and less neon

### Photography Direction

Photography should feel:

- candid
- unposed
- emotionally observed
- intimate without trying too hard

Avoid:

- studio glamour
- heavy retouching
- overly sharp imagery
- excessive styling
- bright commercial lighting

Preferred imagery includes:

- low-light environments
- motion blur
- natural shadows
- reflective surfaces
- empty rooms
- late-night streets
- cars
- mirrors
- smoke/haze
- partial facial visibility
- moments that feel accidentally captured

The imagery should feel like film stills rather than promotional photos.

## Color Direction

### Core Palette

Primary tones:

- black
- charcoal
- off-white
- washed gray

Optional restrained accent tones:

- deep muted red
- desaturated navy
- faded steel blue

Rules:

- low saturation
- no bright colors
- no high-contrast neon palettes
- minimal color usage overall

Texture and lighting matter more than color.

---

## Typography Direction

Typography should feel editorial, refined, and understated.

### Recommended System

- Elegant serif for headlines/titles
- Neutral sans-serif for navigation/body text

Typography should communicate:

- restraint
- confidence
- emotional sophistication
- luxury without flashiness

Avoid:

- trendy distorted fonts
- overly futuristic fonts
- loud typography
- excessive font mixing

The typography should support the mood rather than dominate it.

---

## Website Direction

The website should function less like a corporate artist site and more like an
immersive editorial experience.

### Experience Goals

The site should feel:

- stripped down
- immersive
- atmospheric
- emotionally spacious

The design should prioritize:

- mood
- imagery
- pacing
- negative space

### Structure

Minimal navigation:

- Music
- Video
- Contact

Potential homepage structure:

- full-screen cinematic hero image/video
- sparse text
- understated transitions
- embedded music/video content
- minimal copywriting

Interaction should feel slow and intentional rather than flashy or
hyper-animated.

Avoid:

- clutter
- excessive UI
- busy layouts
- startup/app aesthetics
- aggressive animations
- overexplaining

---

## Brand Personality

The project should feel:

- mysterious
- controlled
- emotionally detached on the surface
- subtly vulnerable underneath

Communication style should be:

- minimal
- selective
- emotionally restrained
- confident without overstatement

The brand should never feel:

- desperate for attention
- performatively edgy
- overly sentimental
- over-explanatory

---

## Strategic Brand Principle

The central tension of the brand is:

> reckless intimacy vs emotional distance

The visuals should carry the vulnerability and atmosphere.

The music and lyrical content should carry the recklessness.

Maintaining this balance is critical to avoiding cliché aesthetics.

---

## Overall Creative Directive

Everything — website, photography, typography, motion, logo, visuals, and
content — should feel like it belongs to the same cinematic world.

The final identity should feel:

- timeless rather than trendy
- emotionally authentic rather than performative
- artistically curated rather than commercially optimized
- minimal but emotionally heavy

The audience should leave feeling like they briefly stepped into Steven
Christopher's private late-night world.
